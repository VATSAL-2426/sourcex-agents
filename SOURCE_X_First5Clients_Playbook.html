Why $997/month specifically — not $1,500

$997 is psychologically below $1,000. For a clinic doing $800K–$2M/year, this is a rounding error. But "under a thousand a month" feels entirely different to a business owner than "fifteen hundred a month." The first three months are designed to prove ROI so decisively that the transition to $1,750 in month 4 feels obvious, not painful. They will not want to leave after seeing the results. This is the strategy.

Why $0 Setup Fee (Not a Discount — A Strategic Decision)

The setup fee is not a profit center at this stage. It is a barrier. Waiving it removes the single biggest objection — "I have to pay $7,500 before I see a result" — without making you look desperate. The framing is critical:

"The setup fee for SOURCE X is $9,500. For the five Charter Partners, it's permanently waived — not as a discount, but because I want my first five clients to judge the system on its results, not on their willingness to take a financial risk on a company that's new. You get the full system. If it works — and it will — you pay $1,750 a month going forward. If it somehow doesn't, you've risked less than $2,000 to find out."

What Makes Them Pay $1,750+ Without Hesitation in Month 4

The Math They See

If the system recovered $4,200 in first-visit bookings in 90 days ($1,400/month average), and they've paid $1,994 total for those 3 months, they're already at 2.1X ROI before month 4 starts.

The conversation about $1,750/month is: "You're paying $1,750 to continue recovering what is effectively $4,000–6,000 per month. The math still works at any price I could reasonably charge."

The Google Review Effect

More Google reviews → higher local search ranking → more organic new patients. A clinic going from 47 to 90 reviews in 3 months is seeing real business impact beyond just the missed calls. This is additional value that compounds invisibly and makes leaving feel genuinely costly.

One new patient from improved SEO = $115 first visit + $600 follow-up LTV. Reviews are not fluff.

The Upsell Path (Month 4 Onward)

Once they are at $1,750/month and happy, introduce the next layer at month 5 or 6:

  • Dormant Patient Reactivation: Send automated SMS/voice to patients who haven't booked in 90+ days. One campaign to 200 dormant patients typically recovers 15–30 bookings. Worth $1,725–$3,450 in one send. Charge $500/campaign or $300/month as an add-on.
  • Multi-Location Expansion: If they have 3 locations on the system, offer location 4 or 5 at $500/month/location instead of a new setup fee. Stacks their existing retainer.
  • Priority Support Upgrade: At $250/month, they get a 2-hour response SLA and a dedicated quarterly deep-dive call. Low cost to deliver. High perceived value to operations-focused owners.

Building Trust Without Case Studies
How to Be Credible Before You're Proven
You cannot fake case studies. But you can build trust through these specific actions instead.
The Live Screen Share
Most powerful trust-builder available
On every discovery call, show the Etobicoke system live. Not a demo environment — the actual running system. Show the dashboard, show the call log, show a real recovered booking. Seeing a live system working is worth more than any case study. No one else pitching them will do this.
Action: Before your first sales call, build a clean demo view of the Etobicoke dashboard. Practice the screen share. Make it 8 minutes max — quick, confident, specific.
PHIPA Documentation First
Send before the sales call, not after
Most vendors bring up PHIPA when asked. Send your PHIPA compliance one-pager in the pre-call confirmation email, before the prospect even has to ask. This signals that you've already thought about what worries them most. For clinic owners, this alone separates you from every other vendor they've ever spoken with.
Action: Create a 1-page "PHIPA Compliance Summary" PDF covering the 4 pillars: consent language, Canadian data residency, interaction logging, data minimization. Include Vatsal's name and contact. Send with every audit.
The Etobicoke Reference
Get permission. Use it fully.
Ask your clinic's owner or director to be a reference — even an informal one. "If a clinic owner I'm speaking with wants to speak to someone who has seen this system work, would you be willing to take a 5-minute call?" Most clinic operators will say yes. One phone reference from a real clinic person is worth 10 written testimonials.
Action: Ask this week. If yes, brief them on what to say: how the system works, what results they've seen, how easy the setup was. Do not leave this to chance.
Operator Credibility Framing
Not a vendor. A peer.
The opening line in every call and every LinkedIn message positions Vatsal as someone who works inside a clinic — not someone selling software to clinics. This is a genuine moat. No US competitor can say "I built this because I saw the problem every morning in my own clinic." Use it constantly and without apology.
Action: Every LinkedIn post, every email, every cold call opens from the operator angle. Never say "our software" — say "the system I built for our clinic."
The Iron Guarantee
Make it uncomfortable for them to say no
The guarantee is behavior-based, not revenue-based: "In the first 30 days, we show you a full report — every missed call responded to, every callback made, every appointment booked. If the system didn't respond to 90% of missed calls within 60 seconds, we work free until it does. If you want out after 60 days, you walk away having paid at most $1,994 total. We can't guarantee how many patients book — we can guarantee the system shows up every time." This is a promise you can keep. Revenue projections are not.
Action: Lead every conversation with the behavior guarantee. Let the revenue data from month 1 speak for itself — don't promise it upfront.
Radical Transparency
Say the thing they're thinking
In every sales call, say this directly: "I want to be upfront — SOURCE X is new. I have one deployment running at a clinic I work in, and five Charter Partner spots I'm opening to clinic owners who want to get in before the price changes. The reason the setup fee is waived is because I'd rather prove results than ask for faith. That is the deal." Saying this builds more trust than any polished pitch. Clinic owners respect directness. They've been sold vaporware before.
Action: Script this paragraph. Deliver it verbatim on every call. Do not soften it.

The One Thing That Kills First-Time Founders

Tweaking the offer instead of doing the calls. Redesigning the audit PDF instead of sending it. Optimizing the LinkedIn profile instead of posting. There is no version of this business that succeeds without 25 dials a day for 90 consecutive days. The offer is ready. The system is ready. The only missing input is volume and consistency. Everything else is procrastination dressed up as preparation.

The 5-Client Success Formula

42 prospects → 25 dials/day → 8 demo calls → 5 closed clients.
