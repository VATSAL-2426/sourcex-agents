## COLD CALL SCRIPTS

### Opening — First Ring

> "Hey [Name], this is Vatsal calling. I actually work inside a 5-location physio clinic
> here in Etobicoke — I'm on the operations side. Quick question for you: is [Clinic Name]
> your clinic?"

*Wait for yes.*

> "Perfect. I built an AI system for our clinic that caught missed calls automatically
> and recovered about [X] in first-visit bookings last month alone. I'm just starting
> to help a small number of other multi-location clinics in the GTA deploy the same thing —
> PHIPA-compliant, plugs into Jane or Juvonno, no front desk retraining.
> Got 90 seconds?"

---

### If They Say "Sure, Go Ahead"

> "Okay great. So the problem we solve is pretty specific: when a patient calls your
> clinic and nobody picks up — peak hours, lunch, after-hours — what happens to that call?
> In most clinics, it goes to voicemail or just disconnects. The patient calls someone else.
> You never knew you lost them.
>
> We put an AI agent on your missed calls. It calls the patient back within 60 seconds,
> speaks naturally, and books them directly into your EMR. No staff involved.
>
> Before I tell you more — quick question: do you have any visibility right now into
> how many calls your clinic misses per week?"

*Pause. Let them answer. This is where you learn everything.*

**If they say "No, we don't track that":**
> "That's the most common answer. And it's the root of the problem — you can't fix what
> you can't see. Here's what I'd like to do: I can run a quick revenue leak estimate
> for your clinic — takes me about 48 hours, it's free, shows you the exact number of
> calls you're probably missing and what that's costing you per month. No pitch in the
> report. Would that be useful?"

**If they say "Yes, we track it":**
> "Interesting — what's your missed call rate? [Listen]. Okay. And what's the callback
> process when a call is missed? [Listen]. Got it. So here's where our system changes
> the math..."

---

### If They Say "We're Not Interested" (First 10 Seconds)

> "Totally fair. Can I ask — is it the AI piece that's the concern, or have you already
> got something handling your missed calls?"

*This forces a real answer. If they hang up, move on. If they answer, there's a conversation.*

---

### If They Say "Send Me an Email"

> "Happy to. Let me grab your email — and can I ask one quick thing before I let you go?
> Is missed-call recovery specifically on your radar, or is the bigger issue the no-show
> problem? I want to make sure what I send is actually relevant to you, not just a
> generic pitch."

*Whatever they say tells you which gap to lead with in the email.*

---

### Voicemail Script (15 seconds max)

> "Hey [Name], Vatsal here — I work inside a 5-location physio clinic in Etobicoke and
> I built an AI system that recovers missed-call revenue. Just starting to help a few
> other GTA clinic owners with the same thing. Worth a quick 90-second call — I'll be
> brief. [your number]. Have a great day."

---

### When They Ask "What Does It Cost?"

> "Setup is $7,500 one-time, then $1,500 a month starting month two. First month of
> the retainer is included.
>
> Here's the math that makes it easy: the average physio clinic in Ontario misses about
> 10% of calls. At your volume, that's probably 5 to 15 bookings a week walking out
> the door. At $110 a first visit, you're looking at $2,200 to $6,600 a month in
> revenue you never see. Our system typically recovers 50 to 70% of that. The setup fee
> pays for itself in 30 to 45 days.
>
> And we put a 60-day guarantee on it — if you don't recover at least the setup fee in
> measurable booked revenue within 60 days, you get it back in full. So your downside
> is zero."

---

### When They Say "We Need to Think About It"

> "Completely reasonable. Help me understand what specifically you want to think about —
> because if it's whether this will actually work for your clinic, that's one conversation.
> If it's the timing, that's different. And if it's the price, I have options.
> What's the real hesitation?"

*They will tell you the real objection. Every time.*

---

### When They Say "We Already Have Something for This"

> "Okay — what are you using? [Listen]. Got it. And is it integrated with your EMR,
> or is it a separate inbox you're monitoring? [Listen].
>
> The reason I ask is most of the clinics I talk to have voicemail or a simple callback
> reminder — but the gap is the speed. If a patient doesn't get a call back within
> 60 seconds, about 65% of them have already called the next clinic on their list.
> Is your current system calling them back that fast automatically?"

---

### Closing the Audit Booking (Not the Sale)

> "Here's what I'd suggest as a first step: let me do a free revenue leak estimate
> for [Clinic Name]. I take your location count, your EMR, your call volume, and
> I put together a 4-page report showing exactly what the three biggest gaps are
> costing you per month. It takes me 48 hours. No pitch inside it — just numbers.
>
> If the numbers don't move you, no harm done. If they do, we book a 20-minute call
> to walk through it together.
>
> What's the best email for you?"

## OBJECTION CHEAT SHEET

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "We're too busy right now" | "The system is designed for that — it runs without adding work to your team. 30 minutes a week for the first 3 weeks while we set it up, then it runs itself." |
| "We're not ready for AI" | "You're already losing revenue to missed calls every day. The question isn't whether to be ready — it's whether you want to know the number first." |
| "PHIPA concerns" | "Every system we build is PHIPA-compliant by design — audit trail, encrypted storage, consent management, data residency in Canada. We've already mapped the compliance requirements for Jane and Juvonno. Happy to walk you through it." |
| "Our front desk already handles this" | "I hear that a lot. Can I ask — what's your current missed-call rate? Most clinics don't track it, which is why this surprises them." |
| "We tried something like this before" | "What did you use? [Listen]. Got it — what broke down?" |
| "Can we do a trial?" | "The 60-day guarantee is effectively a trial with full deployment. You're not paying for a demo — you're paying for a live system with a money-back guarantee." |
| "I need to talk to my partner/partner director" | "Of course. What would make it easiest for them — should I send a one-page summary they can review, or would a 15-minute call with both of you work better?" |
| "Your price is too high" | "Compared to what the system recovers, the ROI is typically 3–5x in year one. But I get it — let me show you the conservative math for [their clinic size]. [Walk through numbers]." |

## DAILY CALL ROUTINE

**Morning (90 minutes, 25 dials)**
1. Pull today's prospects from tracker: `python3 tools/call_tracker.py today`
2. Dial. Log every call immediately after: `python3 tools/call_tracker.py log`
3. Audit requests → generate PDF: `python3 tools/generate_audit.py`
4. Send audit to inbox within 48 hours

**Evening (15 minutes)**
1. Check who responded on LinkedIn
2. Send Message 2 or 3 to appropriate contacts
3. Update tracker: `python3 tools/call_tracker.py summary`

**Weekly (Monday, 20 minutes)**
1. Review pipeline: how many audits sent, how many calls booked, how many close-ready
2. Identify anyone who needs a follow-up push
3. LinkedIn: post one piece of content
