**THE NUMBER THIS MONTH**

**11.**

That's how many calls went unanswered at our Etobicoke clinic between 8:30am and 11am on a Monday morning last month.

Three receptionists. One genuinely busy morning. Eleven patients who called and didn't get through.

At roughly $115 per initial assessment and a 50% booking rate on reached callers, that's approximately $630 in first-visit revenue. In three hours. On one day.

Annualized across similar peak days: that's tens of thousands of dollars in revenue that never entered the booking system and therefore never showed up in any report.

The thing that doesn't show up in your EMR is the revenue you never captured. It literally doesn't exist as a line item.

That's what makes missed-call revenue the most underestimated leak in clinic operations. You can't see it. You have to calculate it.

---

**THE BREAKDOWN**

**What happens to a missed call at most Ontario clinics:**

1. Patient calls during peak hours (8–11am, 12–2pm, 4–6pm)
2. Line is busy or rings to voicemail
3. Receptionist checks voicemail — usually 1–4 hours later
4. Callback attempted — patient has usually moved on or booked elsewhere
5. Outcome: lost patient, $0 revenue captured, no record in the EMR

What should happen:
1. Patient calls, no one answers
2. AI agent calls them back within 60 seconds
3. Speaks naturally, confirms who they are and why they called
4. Books the appointment directly into Jane or Juvonno
5. Sends confirmation. Flags to front desk. Done.

The difference isn't magical. It's timing. Response within 60 seconds vs. response in 90 minutes is the difference between a booking and a competitor's booking.

---

**WHAT I GOT WRONG**

I assumed the highest-value calls would come during business hours.

They don't.

In our first month of deployment, 38% of recovered bookings came from calls that arrived after 6pm or on weekends.

Patients call when the pain is bad. That's often Saturday at 9pm. The AI picked up all of them. I would not have guessed that before we ran the data.

If you have any after-hours call volume, you're likely losing more than you know.
