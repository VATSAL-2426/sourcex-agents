Question 01 of 05 | Missed Calls
Source X
$15,000+
per month

At a 3-location clinic with 60 daily inbound calls and a 10% missed-call rate, that is 6 patients per day with zero follow-up. At $120 per initial assessment, that is $720 per day — over $15,000 per month — evaporating through the phone.

When a patient calls your clinic and nobody picks up — at peak hours, after hours, or when all lines are busy — what happens in the next 10 minutes? In most multi-location clinics, the honest answer is nothing. The patient leaves a voicemail that gets checked hours later, or does not leave one at all. They call the next clinic on their list. That clinic picks up. That clinic books the appointment. Your clinic never knew they lost it.

Honest self-assessment
"Can you pull a report right now showing exactly how many calls your clinic missed last month, and what happened to each one?"
YES — We have a system that tracks every missed call and handles follow-up automatically.
NO — We rely on manual effort, voicemail, or nothing at all.
If you answered NO — the diagnosis

You have a silent revenue leak that compounds daily. At even a modest 5% missed-call rate across your locations, you are losing bookings every single hour your phones are open — and you have no visibility into how many. The revenue does not show up as a line item because you never captured it. It simply never existed in your system. Until you close this gap, you are growing with a hole in the bucket.

The AI Operating System Fix
Module 01

Missed-Call AI Agent — the moment a call is missed, an AI voice agent calls the patient back within 60 seconds. It speaks naturally, captures the reason for calling, and books or re-routes the appointment directly into your EMR. No voicemail. No lost bookings. No staff required.

Source X

per month

At a 5% no-show rate across 100 daily appointments, that is 5 empty slots per day — roughly $450 in lost revenue per day, or over $9,000 per month that is theoretically recoverable with the right same-day automated system in place.

A patient no-shows. The appointment slot is now empty. The therapist is being paid regardless. What does your clinic do in the next 30 minutes to recover that slot? For most clinics: nothing automated. Perhaps a receptionist calls once when they have time. The slot is lost. The therapist sits idle. The revenue evaporates. It happens five times a day, every day, and no one runs a report on the cumulative cost because it feels too normal to measure.

Honest self-assessment
"Do you have a same-day automated workflow that fires the moment a patient no-shows and attempts to rebook the slot before the end of business?"
YES — We have an automated same-day system that recovers no-show slots before we close.
NO — We rely on manual outreach or accept the empty slot as a loss.
If you answered NO — the diagnosis

Every no-show is a recoverable event that your clinic is currently treating as a permanent loss. At industry-standard no-show rates, a 4-location clinic is giving back $9,000–$12,000 per month in recoverable revenue. The slot exists. The patient is still in your system. The only missing piece is a system that acts within minutes, not hours. Without it, you are funding therapist salaries from bookings that never existed.

The AI Operating System Fix
Module 02

No-Show Recovery Engine — the moment a no-show is registered in your EMR, an automated sequence fires: SMS first, then voice follow-up if no response within 30 minutes. Rebook logic attempts to fill the vacated slot before end of business. Repeat no-shows trigger deposit requirements. The slot is recovered without a single manual call from your front desk.

inactive patients

Most multi-location clinics have 500–1,500 inactive patients (defined as no appointment in the last 60 days) in their system at any given time. With no outreach cadence, those patients are available to whoever contacts them first. Usually a competitor.

Your clinic has spent money and time acquiring every patient in your system. Many of them completed a treatment plan, had a life change, or simply drifted away. They are still in your database. They already trust your clinic. Reactivating them costs a fraction of what it costs to acquire a new patient — yet most multi-location clinics have no automated system doing this work monthly. The patients are there. The budget is already spent. The only missing piece is the outreach.

Honest self-assessment
"Do you have an automated monthly outreach campaign running right now to patients who have not booked in 60+ days — personalized by case type, PHIPA-compliant, not a mass blast?"
YES — We have a monthly automated reactivation campaign segmented by patient case type running right now.
NO — We do not have regular automated outreach to inactive patients.
If you answered NO — the diagnosis

You have a database of warm, already-converted patients doing nothing. Each month without outreach is a month those patients are available to whoever reaches them first — and that clinic's reactivation cost is zero, because you already did the hard work of acquiring them. At $10,000–$25,000 per quarter in recoverable dormant-patient revenue for a 4-location clinic, this is the highest-margin gap in your entire operation.

The AI Operating System Fix
Module 03

Patient Reactivation Engine — monthly automated outreach to every patient inactive 60+ days, segmented by case type (back, neck, post-op, MVA, general) with personalized PHIPA-compliant messaging. Not a mass blast. Relevant, timed, compliant outreach that brings dormant patients back before your competitors reach them first.

Question 04 of 05 | After-Hours Intake
Source X

A prospective patient is at peak buying intent in the moment they first reach out. Research on healthcare consumer behaviour consistently shows that patients who do not get an instant response to their first inquiry have a significantly lower likelihood of converting. That first moment is the sale.

A prospective new patient visits your website at 9pm. They want to book or at least find out if your clinic can help them. There is no live chat. The phone goes to voicemail. They close the tab. They may or may not remember to call back tomorrow. They are highest-intent in that first moment. If you are not capturing them then, you are likely losing them to the next clinic that has a 24-hour intake system — and new patient acquisition cost in Ontario is $80–$200 per patient. You just let one walk.

Honest self-assessment
"What happens when a prospective patient tries to contact your clinic at 9pm on a Wednesday? Can they get a response, begin an intake, or book an appointment instantly — without a staff member being present?"
YES — We have a 24/7 intake system that captures and responds to new patient inquiries automatically.
NO — After-hours inquiries go to voicemail or get no response until the next business day.
If you answered NO — the diagnosis

Your clinic's front door is closed 16 hours a day to the highest-intent patients you will ever encounter. Every evening and every weekend, prospective patients are researching, deciding, and converting — just not with you. The competitor who picks up at 9pm wins the booking. You win the voicemail. Over the course of a year, this gap is worth tens of thousands in first-visit revenue that never entered your system.

The AI Operating System Fix
Module 01+

24/7 AI Voice + Intake Agent — the same AI agent that handles missed calls during business hours runs 24/7, capturing after-hours new patient inquiries, completing intake qualification, and booking directly into your EMR. Your clinic never sleeps. Every first-contact moment is captured, every prospective patient gets an immediate response, regardless of the hour.
